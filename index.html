The Course

A rigorous, practical Sports Law curriculum

Covering the essential pillars of sports law — from governance and dispute resolution through to athlete contracts, sports integrity, and the commercialisation of sports rights.

Intro & Overview

What is sports law, sources of sports law, and how sports governance shapes the legal landscape.

2 Lectures

Challenging Sporting Decisions

1 Lecture

Athletes, Contracts & Control

Clean sport and illicit drugs, anti-corruption and gambling, and the regulation of high risk sport and permitted harm.

Teaching Philosophy

Taught by a practitioner,
not just an academic

This course bridges the gap between legal theory and the reality of sports law practice. Every lecture draws on real cases, real disputes, and the practical skills students need to advise clients in the sports industry.

  • Case-driven learning — Students engage with real-world disputes, not hypotheticals
  • Practice-ready skills — Drafting, advising, and problem-solving, not just essay writing
  • Industry perspective — Insights from active legal practice in the sports sector
  • Flexible delivery — Pre-recorded lectures and structured materials suit both online and blended models
