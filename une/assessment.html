Assessment Overview

This course includes two assessment items: a written plea exercise focusing on the Sandpapergate disciplinary scenario, and a final examination. All submissions are made through UNE MyLearn.

Assessment 1

Written Plea of Mitigation or Aggravation

40%
Due
Thursday, 30 April (Week 10)

Prepare a written submission as either a plea of mitigation (if representing the Player) or a plea of aggravation (if acting as Counsel Assisting) in the Sandpapergate disciplinary scenario. You will be assigned a specific player and role.

Assessment 2

Sponsorship Review & Analysis

60%
Format
Invigilated Exam or Take-Home Exam (to be confirmed)
Due
Exam period (to be confirmed)

This assessment requires you to review and analyse a set of commercial sporting documents — a Sponsorship Agreement, a Media Rights Agreement, and a Collective Bargaining Agreement — and respond to a series of questions with reasoned legal analysis.

The task is designed to test your ability to read and interpret real commercial instruments in the context of the governance landscape of a sport. You will need to understand how these documents interact — how the sponsorship sits within the broader commercial and regulatory framework represented by the media rights deal and the CBA — and apply the legal principles covered in the course to answer questions that require careful document review, cross-referencing, and analytical reasoning.

This assessment may be conducted as either an invigilated exam or a take-home exam. The format will be confirmed during the trimester, and preparation resources will be provided in advance.

Academic Integrity

Academic Integrity & AI Use Policy

All assessment submissions must be your own original work. The University of New England takes academic integrity seriously, and so does this course.

You are expected to properly reference all sources using AGLC4 (Australian Guide to Legal Citation, 4th edition). If you are unsure about referencing, consult the AGLC4 guide or ask in the Discussion Forums.

AI tools are permitted for research and outlining only — not for drafting your submission. If you use AI tools in your preparation (e.g., to brainstorm arguments, research case law, or structure your plea), you must acknowledge this in an AI Use Statement at the end of your submission. The words, analysis, and argument in your plea must be genuinely yours.

Do not submit AI-generated text as your own work. Any substantive use of AI-generated content, where not disclosed, constitutes academic misconduct.
