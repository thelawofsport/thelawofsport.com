Comments below are reproduced as submitted by students, with only minor identifying details removed to preserve anonymity. They are grouped into seven recurring themes identified across the survey responses.

1

Practical, Real-World Pedagogy

Law students consistently cite the gap between doctrinal study and legal practice as their biggest frustration. This unit bridges that gap by grounding every topic in the contracts, clauses, and commercial realities that students will encounter from day one in practice.

“

The first subject that enabled us to practically implement the theory we have learned and gave us an insight as to what it would be like to work as a lawyer

“

Mike went through real-world sporting/commercial contracts in the tutorial material… I wish my Contract Law unit went into this much depth to consolidate learning

“

I would recommend this subject to any student who wants to find out how to apply legal concepts and critical thinking in a practical real-life way

“

The focus being on Tutorials and Presentation Slides rather than straight textbook reading shows Michael has focused on the actual nature of Sports Law, being it is based in contract, rather than just historical events

“

This was a very interesting and engaging unit. The structure and practical approach were excellent.

“

This subject was very interesting and made the students see another aspect of sports, instead of just the game and the players. The lecturer provided real-life examples and showed that he had great experience in the area, too.

2

Practitioner-Led Teaching

Students recognise the difference between learning from a textbook and learning from someone who negotiates these agreements for a living. Having an active practitioner at a global sports and entertainment company deliver the unit gives students access to current industry knowledge that no casebook can replicate.

“

To have… the ability to engage with a lecturer of Michael's credentials and to learn the intricacies of Sports Law is truly a unique opportunity

“

The opportunity to ask questions of a man with unequalled qualifications in a field that is going to be significant in legal precedent over the next decade

“

Michael as a practicing lawyer working for one of the biggest sporting/entertainment companies in the world brought invaluable knowledge to the class

“

It was clear that Michael had an extensive knowledge on the subject and he knew how to translate that in a way that was easy to understand and follow

“

It was great to learn from someone who is currently working in the sports law sector

“

I have been involved with elite sports for over twenty years… Before I had sat in on one of Michael's tutorials, I had never realised the contractually layered world under which I had been a party to for all those years. His insights into the ACTUAL legal world revolving around commercial sport, not only domestically, but on the international arena were fascinating to hear first-hand.

Feedback & Student Support

Meaningful assessment feedback is one of the most resource-intensive aspects of legal education — and one of the most commonly cited student complaints across the sector. Students in this unit identified the quality of feedback as a standout differentiator from their other law subjects.

“

The first law subject where I have received feedback that was sufficiently detailed, constructive and genuinely useful for improving future work. Overall, the best taught Law subject I have been enrolled in.

“

Good feedback on students who needed help, always there to answer questions, and went above and beyond to give our assessment results in a timely manner

“

The amount of content certainly covered all basis and the teaching staff were very approachable. The assessment feedback was extensive which really helped understand not just the grade but my application of learning to the assessment.
