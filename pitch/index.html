Teaching Philosophy

Real problems. Real documents. Real practical skills.

This isn't a course that simply talks about sports law in the abstract. Students work with the kinds of documents and problems that practising sports lawyers actually encounter — governance policies, anti-doping codes, player contracts, collective bargaining agreements, broadcasting agreements, and sponsorship deals. The course is designed and taught by a practitioner with significant academic experience, and it shows in every lecture.

  • Real sports documents — Students analyse actual policies, codes, and commercial contracts from the sports industry, not textbook hypotheticals
  • Real legal problems — Every module is built around genuine disputes and scenarios that arise in sports law practice
  • Real practical skills — Drafting, advising, interpreting, and problem-solving — the skills graduates need from day one
  • Industry perspective — Insights drawn directly from active legal practice across governing bodies, leagues, and global sports businesses
  • Flexible delivery — Pre-recorded lectures and structured materials suit both online and blended models

Ahead of the curve: With LPAB reforms from 2028 requiring law degrees to place greater emphasis on practical legal skills, this course is already built around the model that regulators are moving toward — students learning to work with real documents, real problems, and the practical competencies the profession demands.

Taught by a practicing sports lawyer, with significant academic experience.

Michael Bricknell is a practising sports, media and entertainment lawyer and university lecturer who brings real-world legal experience directly into the classroom. Originally from Brisbane and now based in Sydney, Michael holds a Juris Doctor from UNSW and a Bachelor of Economics & Business Management from the University of Queensland.

As Vice President & Counsel at TKO/IMG, Michael advises on some of the largest and most complex commercial arrangements in international sport. Previously, Michael held legal counsel roles at the NRL and Rugby Australia, working at the heart of two of Australia's major professional sporting organisations on integrity, governance, player contracting, and commercial arrangements (including sponsorship, broadcast and licensing).

His career spans the full spectrum of the sports industry — from the governing bodies responsible for the rules of the game, through to global companies that commercialise sporting rights on a worldwide scale.

Michael has over eight years' experience designing, coordinating and lecturing sports law subjects at the university level, and a further eight-plus years teaching contract law. His approach to teaching reflects his background as a practitioner: the course is built around the kinds of legal problems that actually arise in the sports industry — governance disputes, integrity investigations, player contracting, broadcasting deals, and sponsorship agreements.
