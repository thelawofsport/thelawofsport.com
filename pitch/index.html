Teaching Philosophy

Taught by a practitioner,
not just an academic

This course bridges the gap between legal theory and the reality of sports law practice. Every lecture draws on real cases, real disputes, and the practical skills students need to advise clients in the sports industry.

  • Case-driven learning — Students engage with real-world disputes, not hypotheticals
  • Practice-ready skills — Drafting, advising, and problem-solving, not just essay writing
  • Industry perspective — Insights from active legal practice in the sports sector
  • Flexible delivery — Pre-recorded lectures and structured materials suit both online and blended models

Credentials

Your course convenor

Michael Bricknell

Sports Lawyer & University Lecturer

Sports Law University Lecturing Legal Practice Dispute Resolution Sports Media & Broadcasting
▶ Watch Bio Video

Michael Bricknell is a practising sports, media and entertainment lawyer and university lecturer who brings real-world legal experience directly into the classroom. Originally from Brisbane and now based in Sydney, Michael holds a Juris Doctor from UNSW and a Bachelor of Economics & Business Management from the University of Queensland.

As Vice President & Counsel at TKO/IMG, Michael advises on some of the largest and most complex commercial arrangements in international sport. Previously, Michael held legal counsel roles at the NRL and Rugby Australia, working at the heart of two of Australia's major professional sporting organisations on integrity, governance, player contracting, and broadcasting.

His career spans the full spectrum of the sports industry — from the governing bodies responsible for the rules of the game, through to global companies that commercialise sporting rights on a worldwide scale.

Michael has over eight years' experience designing, coordinating and lecturing sports law subjects at the university level, and a further eight-plus years teaching contract law. His approach to teaching reflects his background as a practitioner: the course is built around the kinds of legal problems that actually arise in the sports industry — governance disputes, integrity investigations, player contracting, broadcasting deals, and sponsorship agreements.

How It Works

Simple partnership model

1

We Agree on Terms

Your institution engages Michael for a trimester. We tailor dates, assessment weightings, and branding to suit your program.

2

Your Portal Goes Live

There are two delivery options. The preferred model is a dual-platform approach: students access lectures and course materials through a branded portal at thelawofsport.com, while your eLearning system handles everything else. Alternatively, all content can be built directly within your university's own platform.

3

Sensitive Information Stays With You

Grades, submissions, extensions, student records, and university policies all remain within your eLearning portal. Nothing sensitive sits on an external site — your systems handle what they're designed to handle.
